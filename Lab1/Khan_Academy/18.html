Travel to Kazakhstan

Places for visit:

  1. Baiterek

    2. "Baiterek" full name — "Astana-Baiterek"; kaz. Bayterek is a monument and observation tower in Astana, the capital of Kazakhstan. The monument was built as a symbol of the transfer of the capital from Alma Ata to Akmola in 1997.The project was launched on the initiative of the first President of the Republic of Kazakhstan, Nursultan Nazarbayev.,

  2. Khan_Shatyr

    3. Khan Shatyr (in Russian: mistakenly — "Khan's Tent", correctly — "Khan among tents") is a large shopping and entertainment center in Astana, the capital of Kazakhstan. The opening took place on July 6, 2010 as part of the celebration of Astana Day with the participation of President Nursultan Nazarbayev. A concert by Italian tenor Andrea Bocelli took place at the opening ceremony

  3. Medeo

    4. "Medeo"[2][3], or "Medeu"[4][5] (kaz. Medeu — support; support) is a high—altitude sports complex located in the mountainous tract of Medeu at an altitude of 1,691 meters above sea level, near the Kazakh city of Alma Ata. Medeo is the world's largest alpine complex for winter sports with the largest area of an artificial ice field — 10.5 thousand m2. The highlands and the purest mountain water for pouring ice contribute to achieving high results in speed skating.

Food for eat:

  • Beshbarmak

    • Dish is a crumbled boiled meat[4] with noodles, onion sauce and some special features in the cooking and serving technology, which allows you to achieve the taste inherent in this particular dish[5]. The tradition of finely chopping meat has special significance and ancient roots.

  • Kuyrdak

    • There are many recipes for kuurdak. A common feature of all kurdaks is the roasting of pieces of meat. The Kyrgyz and Uzbek versions usually use meat pulp, fat, and onions[1]. Kuurdak is relatively quick to prepare, so it is usually prepared when it is necessary to serve the dish quickly — if the guests are in a hurry to leave, or before the main dish beshbarmak is ready. Kuurdak can be cooked only from meat (without onions), in this case it is called "Kara kuurdak"
